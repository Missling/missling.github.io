Asking Good Questions

February 22, 2015

In my quest to figure out how to ask a good question, I came across some suggestions that kept reappearing. To start, I define a good question as a question that is likely to be answered. Furthermore, the answer received is relevent to the person that asked.

With tons of questions posted on Stack Overflow, to improve the chance of having your question answered it is best practice to pay attention to your title. The title should be clear, concise and introduce your issue to the audience. Make sure to select the appropriate tag to correctly categorize your question with other similiar questions.

The context of your actual question should expand on your introduction and include a detail description. Depending on the questions, it might be beneficial to include sample code. Always make sure to explain the expected result you want, what you have already tried, and the actual result you got.

Prior to posting your questions, you should go back to proof-read and correct any grammatical errors. As you're re-reading your question, pretend as if you're the audiences and you're seeing the question for the first time. Does the question make sense? Once you received comments, make sure to try it out and provide feedback. This shows respects to the individuals that helped you, and it helps others who have the same issue.
